Visual Hierarchy

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints

https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/

I think this website displays the principle of visual hierarchy by displaying an F-like pattern right when the site loads, which helps the page flow smoothly. It also showcases the grouping of related items in sections, for example, the section about the Book of Mormon, which has a header, informative text, and a button for more related information. The grouping of those sections helps the page flow smoothly as well. The colors, typography, and overall design are consistent throughout the landing page.

White Space and Clean Design

Ableton

https://www.ableton.com/en/

The Ableton site is a place that's familiar to me, and I decided it would be a good example to display white space and clean design. Right from the start, the first part of the lander demonstrates a clean design, closely followed by negative space to guide the user across the site. The use of white space is less prevalent on mobile views, but I believe that it helps keep the design of the site cleaner by keeping the elements from becoming too small or spread out.

Repetition

Georgia Farm Bureau

https://www.gfb.org/

The Georgia Farm Bureau demonstrates the repetition principle of web design by reusing the same designs for multiple elements throughout the page. The first part of the lander reuses the buttons and typography, which helps keep the section consistent. The elements further down share the same designs but have different colors applied to them, keeping the cards distinct but similar. The rest of the elements share familiar patterns with what I've observed.
